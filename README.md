## The Problem

**85% of Indian cities have hard water.** Calcium and magnesium deposits strip your skin barrier after every shower — causing dryness, tightness, and flaking that no regular moisturiser can fix.

Nobody was building for this. Every brand was solving for fairness, anti-ageing, or glow. Nobody solved for the water.

---

## The Market Opportunity

| Signal | Number |
|---|---|
| Indians affected by hard water skin damage | ~500 million |
| India D2C skincare market (2024) | $2.1 billion |
| Growth rate — 2024 alone | 88% |
| HUL acquisition of Minimalist | $350 million (Jan 2025) |
| Whitespace: mid-premium hard-water-specific organic | Zero competitors |

The ₹500–800 mid-premium segment for clinically-positioned, hard-water-specific skincare was completely unaddressed. That's where Quell sits.

---

## Brand Decisions (PM lens)

**Name:** "Quell" — means to calm, soothe, end. One syllable. Trademarkable. "Quell dry skin" is literal search intent.

**Tagline:** "End the dryness." — three words, a command, a brand promise, a hashtag.

**Positioning:** Clinical credibility at mid-premium Indian pricing. Not mass market. Not imported luxury.

**Competitors mapped:** Minimalist, The Ordinary, Dot & Key, Mamaearth, CeraVe — all generic formulations, none hard-water specific.
